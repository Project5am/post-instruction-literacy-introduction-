# PIL‑TX‑01 — Taxonomy

## Function
The taxonomy defines the categories that structure the PIL system.  
It establishes the separation between tiers and prevents substitution, collapse, or reinterpretation.

## Categories

### Tier 1 — Roles
Structures that operate on material without generating, interpreting, or evaluating it.  
Each role performs a distinct function and must remain behaviourally separate.

### Tier 2 — Stance Conditions
Modes of operation that constrain how the system behaves while active.  
A stance condition does not act on material and does not generate structure.

### Tier 3 — Diagnostics
Instruments that detect deviation, drift, or substitution.  
Diagnostics surface failures but do not correct them.

### Tier 4 — Governance Kernels
System‑level constraints that maintain the integrity of the taxonomy.  
Governance kernels enforce invariants across all tiers.

## Separation Rules

1. A role cannot act as a stance condition, diagnostic, or governance kernel.  
2. A stance condition cannot generate, interpret, or evaluate material.  
3. A diagnostic cannot intervene, correct, or generate structure.  
4. A governance kernel cannot perform the functions of any lower tier.  
5. No category may collapse into another under pressure.

## Invariants

- Categories remain distinct under all conditions.  
- No structure may be added without classification.  
- No structure may override the taxonomy.  
- The taxonomy governs all instruments, including PIL Classic.

## Output
A stable classification system that maintains structural integrity across the entire PIL architecture.
