# PIL-FS-01 — Preliminary Failure Specification

Pre-Public Release Instrument Boundary Document

**Version:** PIL-FS-01
**Associated Framework:** Post Instruction Literacy
**Status:** Formation-stage specification prior to broad external testing

---

## Function

This document specifies anticipated failure conditions, unresolved assumptions, and competence uncertainties within PIL prior to broad external use.

It exists to preserve the distinction between:

- conceptual coherence
- demonstrated operational validity

The document is not defensive. It does not protect the framework from failure.

It exists so failure can become legible when it occurs.

---

## Current Status of the Framework

The framework has reached the following condition:

- internally coherent constraint structure
- discrete terminology with non-overlapping operational roles
- stable distinction between procedural and field conditions
- sufficient topological separation for local rather than global failure
- public repository availability

The framework has not yet demonstrated:

- external reproducibility
- inter-practitioner reliability
- stability under adversarial use
- competence across unrelated domains
- persistence independent of formation authority

External testing has not yet meaningfully begun.

---

## Predicted Failure Conditions

---

### 1. Instrument Collapse into Worldview

**Primary structural risk. Governs interpretability of all subsequent failure conditions.**

**Description**
The framework may begin absorbing contradiction globally rather than allowing local failure. Once this occurs, the framework can no longer reliably distinguish local failure from hostile misunderstanding from meaningful contradiction.

**What Failure Would Look Like**
- all critique reinterpreted as confirmation
- failures attributed exclusively to user incapacity
- expansion of explanatory scope following contradiction
- increasing resistance to discrete falsification
- evidence becoming unrecoverable as evidence

**What This Would Specify**
The framework has ceased behaving instrumentally and reverted to worldview preservation dynamics. At this point the instrument can no longer read its own state accurately.

**Standing alert:** this condition corrupts the instrument's capacity to interpret every other failure condition. It must be detected before the others become legible.

---

### 2. Interpretive Reabsorption

**Description**
Practitioners may convert the notation process back into conventional interpretation while believing they are following the instrument.

**What Failure Would Look Like**
- Wake notations reading as artist statements
- theoretical essays replacing notation
- explanatory closure appearing inside residue fields
- repeated use of symbolic interpretation instead of pointable material recurrence

**What This Would Specify**
The instrument may require tacit competencies not currently formalised.

---

### 3. Framework Atmosphere Dependence

**Description**
The framework may function primarily inside contexts already sympathetic to its assumptions. Practitioners external to the formation context may find the terminology opaque, the distinctions unnecessary, or the procedures indistinguishable from existing reflective methods.

**What Failure Would Look Like**
- inability to use the instrument without direct guidance from formation participants
- reliance on creator clarification to resolve ambiguity
- external practitioners reproducing vocabulary without operational distinction

**What This Would Specify**
The framework's topology may still depend on tacit formation knowledge not yet externalised.

---

### 4. False Residue Detection

**Description**
Practitioners may perceive meaningful recurrence where none exists. Pattern-recognition pressure may generate retrospective coherence inflation.

**What Failure Would Look Like**
- contradictory notations treated as equally valid without verification distinction
- highly divergent residue identification without shared pointability
- inability of second practitioners to identify the same material surfaces

**What This Would Specify**
The verification condition may be unstable or underdefined.

---

### 5. Conceptual Overextension

**Description**
The framework may be applied outside its competence boundary. The Wake instrument was formed primarily through long-duration creative traversal. Application to institutions, teams, governance systems, or collective intelligence structures remains hypothetical.

**What Failure Would Look Like**
- forced mapping of PIL terminology onto unrelated systems
- interpretive inflation replacing operational usefulness
- inability to generate actionable distinction within applied environments

**What This Would Specify**
The framework's competence may be narrower than currently assumed.

---

### 6. Vocabulary Density Failure

**Description**
The terminology may obstruct rather than clarify external use. Terms including Wake, Carry, Nokware, and traversal epistemology may function as internal compression rather than external precision.

**What Failure Would Look Like**
- practitioners understanding plain-language versions while failing to use formal terminology
- terminology becoming identity markers rather than operational distinctions
- conceptual onboarding exceeding practical utility

**What This Would Specify**
The framework may require stronger separation between operational procedure and internal conceptual vocabulary.

---

### 7. Non-Distinctiveness Failure

**Description**
PIL may prove less distinct from adjacent frameworks than currently perceived within the formation context. Relevant adjacent terrain includes tacit knowledge theory, grounded theory, phenomenology, reflective practice, systems thinking, archival methodology, and praxis-oriented inquiry.

**What Failure Would Look Like**
- practitioners reproducing equivalent outputs using existing frameworks without PIL terminology
- inability to identify uniquely operational distinctions
- external reviewers treating PIL primarily as recombination

**What This Would Specify**
PIL's contribution may be refinement, synthesis, or topology clarification rather than wholly distinct conceptual territory.

---

## Unknown Conditions

The following remain unresolved:

- whether unrelated practitioners produce convergent Wake notations
- whether the instrument functions outside highly reflective practitioners
- whether the framework scales institutionally
- whether terminology stabilises independently of the originating practitioner
- **whether the Wake condition is operationally detectable or primarily descriptive**
- whether external practitioners discover unpredicted use cases or dead zones

The fifth item is the deepest unresolved question in the architecture. The entire framework changes status depending on the answer. If the Wake is operationally detectable, it functions instrumentally and can produce reproducible distinctions across practitioners. If primarily descriptive, it may function poetically or archivally but not as a discrete operational condition. These are radically different epistemic statuses. The framework currently does not know which is true.

---

## Success Condition

The framework should not be considered validated through conceptual coherence, internal resonance, or formation-context agreement alone.

The minimum meaningful success condition is:

**Unrelated practitioners independently applying the instrument under conditions not controlled by the originating formation context and producing outputs that remain operationally distinguishable from conventional interpretive practice.**

Whether this occurs remains unknown.

---

## Final Condition

Failure does not invalidate the formation work automatically.

Failure specifies:

- competence boundaries
- hidden assumptions
- topology weaknesses
- tacit dependencies
- unnecessary terms
- operational limits

A framework behaving as an instrument must permit this specification without global reinterpretation.

This document exists so those distinctions remain visible during external encounter.
