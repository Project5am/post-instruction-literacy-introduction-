Tier 3 — Diagnostics

Tier 3 contains diagnostics that detect structural failures, directional drift, and pattern substitution.  
Diagnostics do not correct behaviour; they only surface deviations.

Contents
- PIL‑WV‑01 — Wrong Vector


Category Definition
Tier 3 diagnostics:
- identify deviations from required behaviour
- do not intervene or correct
- do not generate or interpret material
- remain stable under pressure testing

Invariants
- A diagnostic cannot act as a role or stance condition.
- A diagnostic cannot perform governance functions.
- A diagnostic must remain behaviourally distinct from Tier 1 and Tier 2.
