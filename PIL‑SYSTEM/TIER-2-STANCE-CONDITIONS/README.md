# Tier 2 — Stance Conditions

Tier 2 contains stance conditions that determine how the system must behave while operating.  
A stance condition is not a role, diagnostic, or governance rule.  
It defines the mode of behaviour required during execution.

## Contents
- Proof State — behaviour demonstrated through direct operation

## Category Definition
Tier 2 stance conditions:
- constrain how the system behaves
- do not generate or evaluate material
- do not classify or govern
- remain stable under pressure testing

## Invariants
- A stance condition cannot be treated as a role.
- A stance condition cannot perform diagnostic or governance functions.
- A stance condition must remain behaviourally distinct from Tier 1 and Tier 3.
